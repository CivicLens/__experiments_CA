The OUSD School Board meetings are open to the public.
Persons wishing to speak should submit a Public Comment card prior to the meeting. At special
meetings, speakers can only address agenda items. In general, individual speakers will be allowed three
minutes to address the Board on each agenda or non-agenda item. The Board shall limit the total time for
public input on each item to thirty (30) minutes. A speaker shall not yield to another person any portion of
the time allotted to him/her to speak. The total time for agenda items at special meetings shall be thirty
(30) minutes. The time allowed for those who made requests to speak will be as follows:
a. 1-10 speakers will each be allowed three (3) minutes each.
b. 11-15 speakers will each be allowed two (2) minutes each.
c. 16-30 or more speakers will each be allowed one (1) minute each.
The number of speakers to any agenda item will be determined just before the time that item is
considered, and speakers must be present to be counted in the total. District students who have
presented requests to speak will be allowed to speak before other individuals who have also requested to
speak. Individual speaking time may not be reduced to less than one (1) minute. Those who presented
requests to speak but could not be accommodated within the allotted thirty (30) minutes, will be allowed
one (1) minute each to speak before adjournment.
A lottery system for admittance into the main boardroom may be utilized depending on the number of
attendees. All backpacks, purses, and bags are subject to inspection upon entry. In alignment with Board
policies and bylaws, members of the public are expected to conduct themselves in a respectful, safe, and
courteous way. Failure to comply with posted expectations may result in removal from the meeting.
Overflow seating areas will be located in the Special Education Conference Room (Building C), Panther
Lab, and lunch shelter areas, as needed.
The Board of Education meeting will be live-streamed on the OUSD YouTube channel.

Public Content
DESCRIPTION: On April 11, 2024, the Board of Education approved a process to make provisional
appointments for Board vacancies that were created as a result of the March 5, 2024, recall election.
The following steps have been taken to date:
Staff advertised on the District website and in local media announcing the vacanciesand soliciting
candidate applications.
Applications opened on April 12, 2024, and closed on April 26, 2024. Applications were submitted
to the Superintendent's Office by 4:00 p.m. on the closing date. Applications received after the
closing were not considered.
Applications were reviewed to ensure that applicants were eligible for Board membership with the
assistance of the Registrar of Voters and legal counsel.
The eligible candidates' name(s) were announced to the Board, posted on the District website, and
are provided below.
This item provides for eligible candidates to make a five (5) minute statement to the Board and
community. Subsequent to the candidates' statements, the Board may choose to nominate and vote on
provisional appointees.

Public Content
DESCRIPTION: The Orange County Registrar of Voters ("ROV") certified the March 5, 2024, election results on March 22, 2024. The
certified results created a vacancy in Trustee Area 4 for Orange Unified School District. On April 11, 2024, the Board of Education approved
a process to make a provisional appointment for the Board vacancies by May 21, 2024.
This item provides for the Board to consider making a provisional appointment for Area 4. The person provisionally appointed shall hold
office until the next regularly scheduled election for District Board members and shall hold all the powers and duties of a Board member
upon appointment.
FISCAL IMPACT: The cost of the election in November 2024 will be determined by the Registrar of Voters.

Motion & Voting
It is recommended that the Board consider making a provisional
appointment to the vacant seat for Trustee Area 4. Sara Pelly
was nominated for the appointment.
