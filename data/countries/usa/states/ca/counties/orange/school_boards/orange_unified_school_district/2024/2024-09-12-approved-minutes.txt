4. ANNOUNCEMENTS AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS
Information, Reports: A. Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Gonzalez expressed gratitude to schools for hosting Patriot Day ceremonies and honoring first responders and
military personnel. He extended condolences to those affected by recent events in Georgia and emphasized the district's ongoing
commitment to student safety, highlighting collaboration with the Orange County Department of Education on threat assessment
training and emergency preparedness. Mr. Gonzalez thanked Mrs. Nicol Jones for her five years of dedicated service on the
Measure S Citizens' Oversight Committee, acknowledging her key role in ensuring transparency in bond project expenditures. He
also addressed the district's response to heat and poor air quality, thanking teams for prioritizing student health, and announced
the Orange High School ribbon-cutting event on October 24. Finally, he expressed optimism about the City of Orange reinstating
the crossing guard program and the district's commitment to finding viable safety solutions.
Information, Reports: B. Board President's Report
Board President Page stated that September is Literacy Month, emphasizing the importance of celebrating the power of
reading and its role in fostering student success. She highlighted that literacy serves as the foundation of learning, critical

thinking, and lifelong achievement, and the district is committed to promoting a love of reading across all grade levels with the
support of teachers and families. Ms. Page also presented the President’s Volunteer Service Award, established by the U.S.
President’s Council on Service and Civic Participation, to six Cerro Villa Middle School students for their contributions to the
Mandarin Immersion Summer Camp. Benjamin Velasco, Alexander Velasco, Elanna Abreu, Eric Grover, Lyla Chan, and Sabrina
Wang assisted elementary students by helping with classroom activities and communicating in Chinese, offering valuable real-life
language practice. She expressed her gratitude to Jennifer Hewit for her efforts in organizing the camp and fostering youth
development.

Information, Reports: G. Board Recognition of Students, Staff and Community
Trustee Erickson welcomed the student board members and shared her pleasure in meeting Miss Tanara from Canyon High
School. She emphasized the importance of their roles as the voices of their campuses and student bodies and expressed
excitement about working with them. Ms. Erickson also congratulated and thanked the Cerro Villa students for their work and
expressed excitement about their recognition by the President of the United States.
Trustee Ortega expressed the importance of keeping in our hearts the families impacted by 9/11 and the recent tragedy in
Georgia. He emphasized the need to remain vigilant and aware of surroundings. Mr. Ortega expressed gratitude for the district's
ongoing staff training, particularly in areas such as active shooter preparedness, and is pleased with the district's direction in that
regard. He also mentioned the positive start to the school year, highlighting the upcoming activities and sports seasons. Finally,
Mr. Ortega thanked everyone, expressing optimism about Orange Unified's continued improvement and fiscal responsibility.
Trustee Pelly welcomed the student board members and expressed admiration for their impressive resumes. She commended
them for taking the time to further serve their school and community and expressed excitement about working with them. Ms.
Pelly also enjoyed the Cerro Villa awards and was proud of the dual immersion program's success, recognizing its continuation
through junior high and high school. Additionally, she gave a special shout-out to the translators and interpreters, emphasizing
the vital role they play. She acknowledged their hard work, from translating documents to interpreting complex IEP meetings,
and expressed deep appreciation. Lastly, she thanked the teachers and staff for managing a tough week dealing with the smoke,
particularly with younger students.
Trustee Yamasaki welcomed the student board members and expressed excitement about working with them. Ms. Yamasaki
noted that school visits by board members have been limited to allow principals and teachers to focus on the start of the school
year and back-to-school nights. She thanked the principals, teachers, and staff for their efforts and expressed her anticipation of
resuming school visits soon. She attended the Nohl Canyon Patriots Day event in remembrance of 9/11 and expressed special
appreciation for the parents, Girl Scouts, and Boy Scouts who participated in the event. Ms. Yamasaki thanked Principal
Strickland for continuing this tradition. Additionally, she highlighted her involvement with various boards, including the Orange
Unified Council of PTA, Education Foundation of Anaheim Hills, Canyon High School Foundation, and the Legislative Coalition,
noting the hard work and dedication of these groups over the summer. She acknowledged the PTA and PTO presidents for their
significant contributions, recognizing the crucial role of volunteers in the district's success.
Trustee Glass welcomed everyone and expressed gratitude to those working with Superintendent Gonzalez and his leadership
team for ensuring a successful school year opening. He specifically acknowledged his neighbor, Phill Martinez, and other parents
for stepping up as crossing guards in front of Palmyra Elementary, emphasizing the importance of safety and appreciating their
efforts. Dr. Glass mentioned his pride in attending back-to-school events at various elementary schools, highlighting the great
work being done. He closed by addressing key stakeholders: encouraging students to absorb their learning, thanking parents for
their trust in the district, and expressing deep appreciation for the hard work of teachers.
Student Board Member Alexander Tran from Villa Park High School reported that their Back to School Night on
September 7th was a major success, with hundreds of students and parents attending to collect honor certificates for those with
a GPA above 3.5. Mr. Tran expressed gratitude to the City of Villa Park and the Orange County Sheriff's Department for their
support during a city e-bike meeting at the school library, where students, parents, and residents learned about responsible
biking. He also mentioned that the school's "Hello Dance" took place on September 6th in the PAC, a change from previous years

when it was held in the quad. The dance serves as a fundraiser for the cheer program. Mr. Tran mentioned that the school will
host a college application event on September 19th and he highlighted the upcoming game against Ayala High School at Fred
Kelly Stadium, with a red, white, and blue theme to honor Patriots Day. For 9/11, He also talked about ASB organizing a letter
drive where students wrote letters to active-duty soldiers and that Hundreds of letters will be sent through the "A Million Thanks"
organization. Lastly, Mr. Tran shared that he now provides city reports at the City of Villa Park meetings.
Student Board Member Nadia Flores from Orange High School expressed her excitement to be present as this year's
Orange High School student board member. She shared that Orange High recently opened its new main office building, which has
brought excitement to the campus. Ms. Flores also mentioned that the school will be hosting an honor roll celebration to
recognize sophomores, juniors, and seniors who excelled during the previous spring semester. She concluded by stating her
honor in sitting beside the board members and her anticipation for a successful year.
Student Board Member Ximena Quijano-Espinoza from El Modena High School shared that the school kicked off the year
with their Hello Dance and Hello Assembly, and recently held an Honor Roll Assembly to recognize students with a GPA of 3.5 or
higher. Ms. Quijano-Espinoza also highlighted that El Modena High is actively engaged in sports, noting a football game at Fred
Kelly Stadium in honor of Lisa Tufua, the wife of the school's librarians and football coach, who recently passed away. She
expressed how honored the school is to celebrate her memory. Ms. Quijano-Espinoza concluded her report by expressing her
excitement to serve and work alongside the board and fellow student members this year.
