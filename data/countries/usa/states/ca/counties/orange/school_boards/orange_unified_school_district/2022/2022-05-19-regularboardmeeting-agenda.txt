Public Content
DESCRIPTION:Implementation of the Local Control Funding Formula (LCFF) and the Local Control and Accountability Plan
(LCAP) regulations require that each districts LCAP public hearing be held in advance and at a separate meeting prior to the
LCAP adoption. The intent of the LCAP hearing is to allow for public comment and recommendations regarding the specific
actions and expenditures proposed to be included in the LCAP and budget (Education Code 52062(b)). Further, the regulations
require that the public hearing for budget be held at the same meeting as the public hearing for the LCAP.
The development and adoption of the LCAPis aState-mandated strategic planning process for districts across the State of
California.Actions and services identified in the eight state priorities are directly aligned to LCFF funding.
The contents of the LCAP has been shared with the Superintendents District Advisory Council (DAC), District English Learner
Advisory Committee (DELAC), and various engaged community partners meetings. An intensive community engagement
process beginning in January2022 resulted in continuous refinement of the LCAP actions and services through use of surveys,
focus group meetings, and consultation with the employee associations.
The publication notification for the 2021-2024Year Two Local Control and Accountability Plan (LCAP) public hearing was placed
in the Orange County Register.The LCAP and budget document is available for public inspection at the District Administrative
Offices.
It is recommended that the Board of Education hold a public hearing and receive public comment on the 2021-2024Year Two
LCAP.
FISCAL IMPACT:As stated in the proposed budget.

Goals :

4.0 Focus Area: Efficient Utilization of Fiscal Capital

Public Content
DESCRIPTION: Proposition 98 regulates school district funding. In 2014, Proposition 2 was passed limiting the levels for
Proposition 98 reserves. If the state reserve levels exceed 3% of the total Proposition 98 expenditures for TK-12, that triggers a
10% cap on local district reserves. If a district has reserve funds exceeding 10%, the overage must be designated for other
purposes, with those purposes specified for the Board to review and approve through a resolution. The 2021-22 state budget act
triggered the reserve cap. Thus, the District, through Resolution 39-21-22, is providing a list of designated uses for the overage
for the Board's approval.

35186 requires school districts to do two things: 1) provide an opportunity to file formal complaints under the Districts Uniform
Complaint Procedures; and 2) report to the Board of Education and the public four times a year the number of complaints filed
and the area of the settlement they target.
The Third Quarter Report covers the number of Uniform Complaints received by the District under the three areas governed by
the Williams Case Settlement for the period of January 1 March 31, 2022. The three targeted areas are:
1. Sufficient Instructional Materials
2. Emergency or urgent facilities conditions that pose a threat to the health and safety of pupils or staff
