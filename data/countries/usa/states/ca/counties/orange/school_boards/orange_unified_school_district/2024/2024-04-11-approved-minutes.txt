5. ANNOUNCEMENTS AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS
Information, Reports: A. Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Gonzalez announced that El Rancho Middle, Santiago Middle, and Canyon High Schools have been recognized as
a California Distinguished School and celebrated the achievement. He also highlighted that Canyon High student Michelle
Setiohardjo is the winner of the National PTA 2023-24 Reflection Award of Merit with her photography piece "Light in the
Darkness". Mr. Gonzalez mentioned he joined the board and many of our schools in the May Parade, hosted by the City of
Orange, along with several other events: the Employee of the Year celebration at Fred Kelly Stadium, the AVID 8th Grade
Standout Students recognition at Angel Stadium, the upcoming Olympiad Games on May 17th, the Retiree Celebration on May
21st, and the Art Exposition on May 30th. He expressed gratitude for the volunteers and their dedicated support of the students
and schools.
Information, Reports: B. Board President's Report
Board President Page welcomed trustees Pelly and Glass, highlighting that their experiences and backgrounds will greatly benefit
our school district and community. She mentioned that teachers were celebrated in unique ways by the students and thanked

each teacher for being the backbone of the educational system and shaping the minds and futures of the students. Trustee Page
spoke about the second annual district-wide elementary Esports competition held earlier in the week. She congratulated Fletcher
Elementary on winning the tournament and presented trophies to the five students.

Trustee Pelly presented the proclamation to Lead Nurse Ms. Haasse. Ms. Haase thanked the board for the acknowledgment and
introduced Ms. Bautista as High School District School Nurse of the Year.

Trustee Page presented the proclamation to Mr. Goodlander, OUEA President and teacher at Orange High School. He thanked the
board for the acknowledgment.
Information: E. Board Proclamation: Classified School Employee Week – May 19-25, 2024
Trustee Glass presented the proclamation to CSEA Chapter President Ms. Espere.
Information, Reports: F. Board Recognition of Students, Staff and Community
Trustee Erickson mentioned that she attended the AVID senior standout event at UC Irvine and congratulated Orange Unified
standouts Natalie Hernandez from Canyon High, Halburt Nguyen from El Modena High, Rosalyn Aguirre from Orange High, and
Victoria Chavira from Villa Park High. Ms. Erickson also attended Running Springs where sixth grade IB students presented
impressive research reports, as well as Santiago's performance of High School Musical, The Lion King performed by Chapman
Hills, a Cinco de Mayo community celebration at Esplanade, and visits to Anaheim Hills and Yorba, and the Employee of the Year
celebration. She thanked the teachers for their service and dedication to Orange Unified students.
Trustee Yamasaki recognized all teachers, educators, and classified staff in Orange Unified for their exceptional dedication. She
highlighted her participation in the May Parade, where many Orange Unified Schools were represented, and enjoyed seeing the
community come together. Ms. Yamasaki appreciated the Distinguished School celebration for Canyon High, Santiago Charter,
and El Rancho Charter. She particularly enjoyed the event at Canyon High School organized by the administration for the
students. Additionally, Ms. Yamasaki joined Ms. Erickson in visiting the Running Springs Exhibition, which showcased the learning
experience of students in the IB program.
Trustee Pelly thanked everyone for the kind words and expressed her excitement about beginning her service. She mentioned
her enjoyment of the parade and the Employee of the Year celebration. Ms. Pelly congratulated the Fletcher Esports team and
thanked the coach for their excellent work. She highlighted that the first Fletcher Mandarin Immersion cohort, who are now
juniors and were only third graders when she was their principal, took their AP Chinese Language and Culture exams on this day.
She congratulated the students and expressed her enthusiasm for all the remarkable programs at Orange Unified.
Trustee Glass thanked the public for their trust, expressing his hope to bring positivity, unite the educational community, and
integrate the diverse voices and visions that represent Orange Unified students and stakeholders. He conveyed his humility and
honor in serving as a new trustee, considering it a privilege. Dr. Glass also stated that he welcomes all feedback.
Trustee Rumsey expressed her excitement for the Elmo Jam event, where students performed with their bands and sold crafts
and plants. She congratulated the employees honored at Fred Kelly Stadium and thanked all Orange Unified staff for their
dedication. Ms. Rumsey mentioned attending her first LedgeCo meeting, where Senate Bill 291 about recess was discussed,
which will go into full effect in the 2024-2025 school year. She explained that she fully supports the right for a child to have
recess and does not support taking this away as a form of punishment.
Trustee Ortega announced that the Orange High School Band received the John Williams Award at the May Parade, achieving
first place among all bands that competed that day. He also mentioned that the OC Register covered Villa Park High School girls'
swim team winning the CIF Championship.
Student Board Member Jiya from Canyon High School announced that 11 Canyon High School athletes signed Letters of
Consent on athletic signing day. She also mentioned that Boys' and Girls' Lacrosse, Softball, Baseball, and Boys' Volleyball teams
all qualified for CIF. She was impressed with the student-choreographed performances by the Canyon High dance teams. Ms.
Patel expressed pride in the student section for earning their first-ever title as North Orange County Divisional Champions,
ranking Canyon's school spirit at number one in North Orange County. She also talked about the special celebration organized by
Principal McKee featuring an extended lunch and treats in honor of the Distinguished High School award. Ms. Patel mentioned
that it's AP testing this week, PROM will be held on Saturday, and the Choir Showcase will take place later in the month.
Student Board Member Emma Hoffmann from El Modena High School talked about Atheltic Night for incoming 8th
graders, held last month to introduce them to the school's athletic programs. She mentioned that many fun senior activities are
happening next month, including senior signing day, the goodbye assembly, as well as Prom with a "Night in Paris" theme. Ms.
Hoffmann noted that the Track Team is the league champion and that 5 boys and 8 girls made it to CIF. She also expressed pride
in the Baseball and Softball teams winning their league and moving on to CIF. She highlighted that Teacher Appreciation Week
was celebrated with the AP Environmental Science class making small succulent plants for each teacher.
Student Board Member Nancy Albeno from Orange High School talked about the last blood drive of the year where many
seniors donated. She highlighted the May Parade led by the Orange High JROTC, in which the school's cheer team tied with
Santiago Middle for most spirit, Orange High's talented band won an award, and where she joined the parade as Princess Leia.
Ms. Albeno enjoyed the #Accept the Day, organized by the College and Career Center where all seniors were celebrated. She
explained that the school held a Dome Celebration, as a final hurrah to the historical dome on campus. Ms. Albeno expressed her
pride for the softball team, which made it to CIF for the first time in two decades. She mentioned that for teacher appreciation
day, ASB distributed baskets and wrote letters to all the staff on campus.
ASB Member Marianna Mendoza from Villa Park High School stated that Villa Park had its Campus Care Day in April, which
involved painting walls and adding new soil. She mentioned that the Orchestra and Band collaborated with local middle and
elementary schools and that the choir performed at the SCVA festival. Ms. Mendoza talked about the celebration of the AVID
Stand Out ceremony and congratulated Victoria Chavira on receiving the honor. Other events she mentioned were the makeover
of the mural, the recognition of the mock trial team, and the academic competition teams by the City of Villa Park Council for
their success, and the students versus teachers dodgeball tournament. She congratulated Villa Park Student Nathan Nala for
being selected as a semi-finalist for the OC Register Artist of the Year and receiving an award from the Constitutional Right
Federation Orange County for his role as an attorney in the mock trial team. Ms. Mendoza also mentioned the last club rush of
the year, the great performance of "Mama Mia" by the theater program, the celebration of Teacher Appreciation Week, the May

Parade, and the jazz bands who took two first-place trophies home at the Fullerton College Jazz Festival. She expressed her pride
in the girls' swim team for winning CIF Division Two.
