4. ANNOUNCEMENTS AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS
Information: A. School Spotlight: Alexander Tran - Villa Park High School
Student Board Member Alexander Tran from Villa Park High School expressed gratitude to the choir for singing the National
Anthem. He also thanked Principal McCuistion, Activities Director Ms. Scott Sawyer, and his ASB class for joining him tonight.
Alex talked about the Spartan apprenticeship program, where students are assigned to committees to learn the way of student
leaders and assist them in achieving success both on and off campus. He explained that students learn how to plan dances,
prepare sports assemblies, and contribute to the Spartan newsletter. Alex reported on the success of two club rushes, which
raised several thousand dollars, and the noticeable increase in student organizations. He highlighted the successes of artistic
programs like Choir, Color Guard, Pom team, and theater program, all of which contribute to spreading the Spartan spirit in the
community and on campus. A video showcasing the school year was presented.
Information, Reports: B. Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Gonzalez talked about Measure S, a bond approved in 2016 to fund facility modernization and construction
projects at the four comprehensive high schools. As a result of our Phase I projects, OUSD students and teachers are utilizing
just over 200,000 square feet of modern teaching and learning space across the campuses at Orange, Villa Park, Canyon, and El
Modena High Schools. El Modena and Canyon also had HVAC units installed, with a helicopter being utilized at Canyon due to site
logistics. A video was shown on placing the HVAC system with the use of a helicopter. Mr. Gonzalez explained that Phase II will
provide additional campus improvements specific to each school siteâ€™s needs and that the next video update will be shared after
our new Senior Executive Director, Facilities & Planning, Isela Vazquez, gets settled in.
Information, Reports: C. Board President's Report
Board President Ortega welcomed everyone back. He mentioned that he looked forward to working with the students and the
parents. He stated that he wants parents fully involved in the education of their students. He also mentioned the 7-11 committee
which will be brought forward on the website and that he shared this with Legislative Committee members. He noted that
misinformation is being spread about the recall and that he is looking into legal repercussions. Trustee Ortega suggested keeping
item 4D, Board Recognition of Students, Staff and Community short.

break permanent shade structures were installed, a welcome surprise for which she expressed gratitude.
