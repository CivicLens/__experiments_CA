4. ANNOUNCEMENTS AND ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS
Information, Reports: A. Superintendent's Report
Superintendent Gonzalez shared that Orange High's new 26,000-square-foot student services and administrative building,
featuring six new classrooms and the historic Townsend Room, is nearly complete. He highlighted campus improvements
including an alumni corner, expanded main quad, updated lighting, new landscaping, and added walkways. Mr. Gonzalez
presented a video showcasing the project's progress. He also welcomed Sulema Holguin as the new assistant superintendent of
business services, highlighting her extensive 27-year career in public education.

Information, Reports: B. Board President's Report
Board President Page shared that the facilities team utilized the summertime to update classrooms and facilities, completing
projects such as new fencing, recertification of fire alarms, adding room numbers to classes, installing new roofs at five schools,
resurfacing blacktops at two schools, creating a new playground at the Child Development Center, replacing HVAC systems, and
adding more shade structures at elementary schools. She expressed her excitement for the students who will enjoy these
upgrades. Ms. Page also discussed the extended school year for students with IEPs, highlighting how many students were excited
to come to school every day and socialize. She mentioned visiting Learning Links, a program for caregivers and students aged 0
to 5, which offers various developmental activities at different stations. This program is held at Palmyra and Handy Elementary
until August 1st, with additional sites planned for morning and afternoon sessions throughout the school year. Ms. Page talked
about the Mandarin Language Summer Camp, where students learned the language in the morning and participated in cultural
activities like dance, calligraphy, and yoyo in the afternoon. She concluded by expressing her enthusiasm for the new school
year.
Information, Reports: C. Board Recognition of Students, Staff and Community
Trustee Yamasaki thanked the numerous volunteers who assisted the Canyon Education Foundation at the fireworks booth.
She encouraged everyone to join the PTA, emphasizing its positive impact on schools and students. She also mentioned visiting
the Mandarin Summer Camp at Fletcher Elementary, expressing amazement at the student's language skills and enjoyment of
the dances and calligraphy demonstrations. Ms. Yamasaki extended her gratitude to the Global Culture Education Foundation for
sponsoring the camp, as well as to the teachers and volunteers who made it possible.
Trustee Pelly expressed her excitement about the extensive work completed over the summer by the construction and
maintenance departments. She enjoyed following the summer programs on social media, noting that these programs aim to
prevent learning loss while ensuring students have fun. She thanked everyone involved in organizing these programs to help
students avoid learning loss.
Trustee Glass shared that he enjoyed his first July 3rd celebration at Grijalva Park. He emphasized the importance of student
involvement in sports and other district activities, highlighting the invaluable interactions between students. He expressed his
appreciation for all the positive developments in the district.
Trustee Erickson thanked the communications department for the Measure S update presentation. She also expressed
gratitude to the community and volunteers who worked tirelessly for years to pass Measure S, and she is pleased to see it come
to completion. She extended her thanks to the volunteers, taxpayers, and construction companies as our students deserve to
have state-of-the-art facilities.
Student Board Member Alex Tran from Villa Park High School reported on the extensive construction at the school, including
updates to the gym and the Measure S Phase 2 building for English and Math. He also mentioned that ASB and student
organizations are preparing for the upcoming school year's registration.
